## About the role
We’re building ICU monitoring + diagnostics devices. You’ll own PCB design end to end: schematic, layout, vendor coordination, bring up, debug, and documentation. This role is for someone who’s comfortable getting their hands dirty in the lab and shipping boards that behave in the real world (noise, power, EMI, manufacturing).

## What you’ll do
1) Own schematic design for mixed signal embedded systems (MCU, sensors, ADCs, comms, power)
2) Do PCB layout in Altium (or KiCad) with good grounding, return paths, stack up, and placement discipline
3) Work with embedded/firmware to define interfaces, debug hooks, and test points
4) Support bring up and debug: power rails, clocks, buses (I2C/SPI/UART), signal integrity issues, intermittent faults
5) Design for reliability and manufacturability: DFM checks, panelization inputs, connector choices, assembly constraints
6) Coordinate with PCB fab and assembly vendors: stack up, impedance needs, lead times, yield issues
7) Basic EMI/EMC thinking from day one: filtering, shielding strategy, layout habits to avoid pain later
8) Maintain documentation: BOM, revision control, schematics, assembly notes, test procedures

## Must have
1) 2 to 10 years experience in PCB design for shipped products
2) Strong schematic + layout skills in Altium (preferred) or KiCad
3) At least one end to end shipped board that you took through bring up and debug
4) Comfortable with mixed signal fundamentals (noise, ADC considerations, grounding)
5) Practical power design experience (DC DC selection, LDOs, protection, sequencing basics)
6) Can collaborate cross functionally and communicate trade offs clearly

## Nice to have
1) Medical device experience or other regulated products
2) Exposure to EMI/EMC pre compliance, filtering, and fixing failures
3) Experience with battery powered systems and charging
4) Experience working with high density boards (fine pitch, BGAs, controlled impedance)

## What “good” looks like in 30 to 60 days
1) You’ve taken ownership of an existing board or new revision and improved reliability or manufacturability
2) You’ve shipped a prototype PCB iteration with clean schematics, layout, and a buildable vendor package
3) You’ve set a clear bring up checklist and debug flow that speeds iterations

## Interview / evaluation focus
Portfolio deep dive (boards shipped), a quick schematic review exercise, and a bring up/debug discussion

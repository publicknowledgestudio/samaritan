## About the role
We’re building ICU monitoring + diagnostics devices. This is a senior hands on mechanical role owning end to end NPI: concept, architecture, detailed design, simulation, vendorization, and first builds. You’ll be the person who makes the product manufacturable and robust, not just “nice in CAD.”

## What you’ll do
1) Own mechanical system design for ICU monitoring + diagnostics devices: enclosure, structural, thermal, mounting, cable routing, sealing, serviceability
2) Drive concept to production: requirements, architecture, detailed CAD, drawing packs, tolerances, materials, finishes
3) Lead DFM/DFA and cost down with vendors; take parts from prototype (3D print/CNC/sheet) to tooling (injection molding) where needed
4) Plan and execute simulation to de risk designs: FEA (strength, stiffness, vibration), CFD/thermal where relevant; interpret results and iterate designs
5) Own vendor management for mechanical: RFQs, selection, capability checks, process limits, timelines, quality expectations
6) Support build and debug: first article inspection, fit issues, root cause, corrective design changes, ECOs
7) Set engineering discipline: BOMs, rev control, documentation, design reviews, tolerance stack approach
8) Collaborate tightly with EE/embedded for PCB integration, EMI constraints, connectors, sensor placement, thermal paths, assembly sequence

## Must have
1) 7 to 15 years building physical products, ideally electromechanical
2) Strong SolidWorks (or equivalent) and drawing release discipline
3) Real DFM experience with vendors (machining, sheet metal, injection molding); you’ve shipped at least 2 products end to end
4) Working knowledge of simulation (ANSYS/COMSOL or similar): you know what to simulate, what assumptions are dangerous, and how to translate results into design changes
5) Strong tolerance thinking (GD&T, stack ups) and assembly mindset
6) Comfortable owning schedules and pushing through ambiguity

## Nice to have
1) Medical devices or regulated product exposure
2) Experience with IP ratings, sealing, and reliability testing
3) Experience building fixtures/jigs/test rigs for manufacturing
4) Experience with thermal management around sensors, power electronics, or compact enclosures

## What “good” looks like in 30 to 60 days
1) You’ve audited current mechanical design and defined the path to a vendor ready build
2) You’ve run at least one simulation driven design loop that materially reduces risk (thermal/structural)
3) You’ve set a clean release process: drawing packs, BOM, revs, ECO flow

## Interview / evaluation focus
Portfolio review (designs you shipped), a practical DFM deep dive, and a short simulation reasoning exercise (what would you simulate and why)

## Compensation
Competitive, based on level; includes meaningful ownership for the right person

## About the role
We’re building ICU monitoring + diagnostics devices. You’ll build production grade firmware that runs in the field: device drivers, comms, reliability, logging, OTA updates, and debugging. This is not a “toy IoT” role. We care about uptime, safety, and crisp engineering.

## What you’ll do
1) Write and maintain embedded firmware in C/C++ for ESP32 and or STM32 class MCUs
2) Build RTOS based systems (FreeRTOS): tasks, queues, timing, memory constraints
3) Implement and maintain OTA update flows, versioning, rollback strategy, and release discipline
4) Bring up new hardware with the EE team: peripherals, sensors, buses (I2C/SPI/UART), ADCs, interrupts
5) Build reliability features: watchdogs, brownout handling, fault logging, crash dumps, safe modes
6) Implement comms as needed (BLE, WiFi, UART, CAN, or similar), and integrate with a backend or mobile app if applicable
7) Create test hooks and basic manufacturing test firmware routines
8) Document APIs, build steps, and debugging playbooks so the team can move fast

## Must have
1) 3 to 12 years experience shipping embedded firmware in production
2) Strong Embedded C (and ideally some C++) with good debugging habits
3) Experience with FreeRTOS or another RTOS in real products
4) Hands on experience with at least one of ESP32 or STM32 (both is a plus)
5) You’ve shipped firmware that ran in the field and handled real world failures
6) Comfortable working in the lab: scopes, logic analyzers, debugging weird hardware software interactions

## Nice to have
1) OTA at scale, robust bootloaders, secure update practices
2) Medical devices or other regulated products
3) Experience with power optimization for battery powered devices
4) Exposure to unit testing on embedded, CI pipelines, or hardware in the loop testing

## What “good” looks like in 30 to 60 days
1) You’ve stabilized one critical firmware area (crashes, memory leaks, comms reliability) and made it measurable
2) You’ve shipped an OTA capable firmware release with clean versioning and rollback
3) You’ve improved bring up speed by adding better debug tooling, logs, and test points requirements

## Interview / evaluation focus
Debugging deep dive (real incidents you fixed), a short RTOS reasoning exercise, and a hardware bring up scenario
